Subscriptions, Alerts & Lifecycle

Subscribe, get notified, triage the change, close the loop — and keep a full audit trail throughout

~4:30
estimated duration
Target: consumer teams, API producers, engineering leads
Scene 1 — Subscribing to a service0:00 – 0:40

On Screen

Open a service detail page. Click Subscribe — button changes to Subscribed ▾. Open the dropdown: Breaking changes only / All changes toggle, and an Unsubscribe option. Switch to the My Subscriptions nav page — shows the service row with its preference chip.

Voiceover

"Subscribing is a single click from the service page. Once subscribed, the button becomes a dropdown — you can switch between breaking-changes-only and all changes, or unsubscribe, without going anywhere else. And the My Subscriptions page gives you one place to see and manage every service you follow."

Scene 2 — The alert email0:40 – 1:20

On Screen

An email client opens. Subject: "[SpecVault] Breaking changes in Payments API v2.1.0". Body: service name, version jump 2.0.0 → 2.1.0, 2 breaking changes table (method, path, description, field), blast-radius line "impacts payments@acme", link to the change in the portal.

Voiceover

"The alert email carries everything you need without opening the dashboard: the version that changed, every breaking change in a table with method, path, and field, and the blast-radius summary naming the affected consumers. There's a direct link to the change event if you need the full context."

Scene 3 — Change event triage: Acknowledge1:20 – 2:05

On Screen

Open the Changes tab. A change event with status badge New. Click Acknowledge — badge flips to Acknowledged in blue. Tooltip/label: "Intentional — consumers have been notified." Show the audit log entry: change_acknowledged with timestamp and user.

Voiceover

"When a breaking change is intentional — a planned deprecation, a known schema migration — click Acknowledge. The status updates to Acknowledged, the alert has done its job, and the audit log records who closed it and when. No confusion about whether it was reviewed."

Scene 4 — Triage: Flag as error, then fix it2:05 – 3:00

On Screen

A second change event — click Flag as Error. Badge becomes Fix Pending in amber. The producer team publishes a corrective version 2.1.1 — the spec restores the removed field. Immediately: the Fix Pending badge flips to Resolved in green. Audit log: change_auto_resolved.

Voiceover

"When a breaking change was a mistake, flag it as an error — the status becomes Fix Pending, signalling the team it needs a correction. When the producer publishes a corrective spec that reverts the change, SpecVault auto-resolves the flag. No manual close. The full sequence — flagged, fixed, resolved — is in the audit log."

Scene 5 — Deactivate a service3:00 – 3:40

On Screen

On the service detail header, click Deactivate. The service name gains an Inactive badge. The service row on the home page is greyed out. Publish a new version — no email fires. Click Reactivate — badge disappears, alerts resume on the next publish.

Voiceover

"When a service is winding down or in maintenance, deactivate it. SpecVault still records change events — the full history is preserved — but no alerts fire. Reactivate when you're ready and alerts resume immediately. Deactivate is not delete: the service, its versions, and its audit trail are all still there."

Scene 6 — Audit trail3:40 – 4:10

On Screen

Open Audit Log from the nav. Rows: service_created, spec_published, change_acknowledged, change_flagged, change_auto_resolved, service_deactivated. Each row shows timestamp, actor, and event type. Filter by service or event type.

Voiceover

"Every significant action — spec published, change triaged, service deactivated — writes an audit entry. Who did it, when, and what it was. So when an incident asks 'did anyone acknowledge this before it shipped?' — the log has the answer."

Scene 7 — CTA4:10 – 4:30

On Screen

Lifecycle recap: Subscribe → Alert → Triage → Resolve → Audit. URL: specvault.sentinelflux.in

Voiceover

"Subscribe to what matters, get alerted when it breaks, triage the change, and close the loop — with a full audit record throughout. That's the full lifecycle. specvault.sentinelflux.in."
